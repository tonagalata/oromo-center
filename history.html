Heritage

The Oromo People

The Oromo are one of the largest peoples in Africa, united by the Afaan Oromo language and a shared cultural system centered on the democratic Gadaa institution.

The Gadaa System

A UNESCO-recognized indigenous democratic system organizing social and political life in age-sets and eight-year cycles, emphasizing consensus, accountability, and rule of law.

Cultural Traditions

Rich music, dance (shaggoyee), poetry, and oral traditions sustain identity across regions and the global diaspora.

The Oromo originate in the Horn of Africa, predominantly in Oromia. Oromo communities also live in northern Kenya and parts of Somalia. Afaan Oromo is among Africa's most widely spoken languages and anchors Oromo identity worldwide.

Historically, the Oromo expanded through peaceful integration and complex social systems. Despite periods of marginalization, Oromo culture, language, and institutions have endured and flourished at home and in the diaspora.

Purpose

Our Mission & Vision

Mission

To empower the Oromo diaspora by providing essential services, preserving cultural heritage, and fostering community connections that help individuals and families thrive in their new communities while maintaining strong ties to their roots.

Vision

A united, thriving Oromo diaspora community that maintains its rich cultural identity while fully participating in and contributing to the diverse societies where its members live, with future generations proud of their heritage and equipped for success.
